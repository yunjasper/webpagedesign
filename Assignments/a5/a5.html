Finally, a word of caution. Linking documents from a framed page can be confusing and misleading. For example, if your document links to a remote site on the web, the linked document, by default, will open inside your frame!. This is called "capturing" a site in a frame, and for this and other reasons, some experts still consider frames a controversial topic.
This default may be exactly what you want, if you are linking to one of your locally prepared pages. But if you are linking to a remote page that is not your work , then place the target="_blank" attribute and value with your href. This will display in a new page OR a new tab of the same window (depending on setup of the browser), as it should.
